BUSINESS VALUE OF A CHATBOT

A chatbot is a modern alternative to business communication that can unify all business applications and provide a bridge to “talk” between the enterprise and its users/customers in this messaging age.

A chatbot is an interactive platform that is a smart and friendly virtual assistant that combines an omnichannel communication experience into one simple interface, removes the need to navigate across various applications for repetitive tasks and simplifies the entire engagement experience into a contextual conversation.

Automating most human centric processes through bots also gives a company an image of being forward-looking and technology-oriented. Adopting such a platform unlocks the business’s ability to rapidly release functionality previously unheard of because of technical, financial, personnel restrictions. For example, an On demand self-service bot can address time cards, password resets, document retrieval, FAQ, etc.).

As we move towards this messaging age, business needs to adapt to consumers increasing want to have a closer relationship with them as well as being easier to engage with. Chatbot allows customers to chat in a channel agnostic (Web App, Mobile App and Chat Service) way as well as provide in some cases a better outlet that is impersonal i.e. HR questions, Re-occurring engagements, etc.

  • The Bots talk the same language as your users

  • Channel Agnostic Experience. Allows customers to Interact through native messaging channels/mobile apps/web apps and have a secured route through to your enterprise applications.

  • An impersonal touch - ask those questions which you were afraid to speak to a person, pester the Bot as much as you want.

  • Chatbots can also provide immense scope for companies to improvise and learn consistently about their customers.

  • Virtual Assistants powered by Artificial Intelligence helps streamline processes better by directing a client to a chatbot to respond to basic queries from a customer, and then escalate them seamlessly to email or a phone call if required. Such engineered smart processes can help deliver better customer satisfaction and, not to mention, free up agents’ time in the process.

What is a BOT?

A chatbot is a computer program which conducts a conversation via auditory or textual methods. Such programs are often designed to convincingly simulate how a human would behave as a conversational partner, thereby passing the Turing test. Chatbots are typically used in dialog systems for various practical purposes including customer service or information acquisition. Some chatterbots use sophisticated natural language processing systems, but many simpler systems scan for keywords within the input, then pull a reply with the most matching keywords, or the most similar wording pattern, from a database.

Types of Bots

  • NLP or intent based chatbot – a chatbot that is based on statistical probability, it uses a library of communication languages (English, slang etc.) to “estimate” what the consumer is trying to say. This will increase the likelihood that customer may ask any question, the bot will understand the intent and respond. Example Oracle

  • Humane chatbot – a chatbot that’s purpose is to boil the ocean, to learn all that can be asked and provide an answer. As a result, the chatbot answer many questions from football to weather but as a result is pricey and does not adhere to business needs. To expand on this point an example would be if I have a domino chatbot would a consumer ask about the weather? If they did this, what business value does answering irrelevant questions provide back to the organisation? Example Watson

  • Rule based chatbot :adopts the methodology of burning every possible way a question can be asked and match it to an answer. A very difficult task considering any punctuation, spelling mistake, synonyms of every word will be tried and if not found exactly matching then will return an error. This approach is simple in implementation and will always return correct result if asked the right question. This implies a person may need to train on how to use the chatbot if the chatbot is not exhaustive enough. Example Tay
